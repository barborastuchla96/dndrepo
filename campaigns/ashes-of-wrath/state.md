What he knew and didn't say: The Order briefed three Guild factors twenty years ago on the chip system as insurance. Moor's predecessor was one of them. Moor has known about the chips since before the purge. He couldn't stop it — moved too fast. Has thought about it every day since. Truth-sense: complete truth.

What he's providing:
- Guild transit to Thessan IV and Kael's Rest — off books
- Early warning network if Vorne moves before the operation concludes
- Credited (not controlling) role in the release
- Cael's location: **Heth's Crossing, Compact holding facility, alive**

What was set clearly:
- The release is the team's. He's credited. He does not control it.

**Moor is now a known quantity.** Not fully trusted — but fully understood. That's better.

## DM Notes
- Thessan IV: The territorial war between House Merrath and House Solenne has created a genuine humanitarian crisis. Refugee camps outside the main settlement. The sanctum ruins are in a contested buffer zone — neither house controls it, which is why the chip survived, but access is dangerous.
- Lysa Vorne runs a medical tent in the largest refugee camp. She's good — precise, tireless, her mother's discipline turned toward healing instead of harm. The team will need medical supplies or assistance before they can reach the ruins, which brings them to her camp. She introduces herself as Lysa. Last name doesn't come up immediately.
- The chip four retrieval requires navigating a live conflict zone. No social infiltration this time — physical danger, real stakes.
- Maren will recognise something in Lysa eventually. Not the face — she's never met Cassia Vorne in person. Something in the precision. The stillness. She'll say nothing until after the chip is retrieved.
- Davan on Thessan IV: he functions well in crisis. The refugee camp brings out something in him — the archivist's instinct to document, to witness, to make sure things are recorded. He'll ask if he can spend time with the camp's unofficial record-keepers. Let him. It matters.
- After Thessan IV, before Kael's Rest: Davan will be ready. He'll say so simply, without drama. *I'm ready.* Believe him.
- When all seven chips are assembled the Remainder speaks directly — not through prescient flashes, not through resonance. Direct contact. What it says will reframe everything the team thinks they know about why the Order was really founded and what Seravyn's role in what comes after actually is.
- Cael at Heth's Crossing: he's being held as leverage, not for information — Vorne thinks someone will come for him. She's right, but not on her timeline. He's been kept in reasonable condition because a dead hostage is worthless. When the team arrives he'll be disbelieving, then furious at himself for being caught, then quietly overwhelmed that it worked.
