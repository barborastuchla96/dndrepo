## Personality Generation

### Quick Traits (d6 each)

**Demeanor**:
1. Nervous, fidgety
2. Gruff, curt
3. Warm, welcoming
4. Suspicious, guarded
5. Arrogant, dismissive
6. Weary, resigned

**Motivation**:
1. Survival—just getting by
2. Greed—wealth above all
3. Loyalty—serving someone/something
4. Ambition—climbing higher
5. Revenge—settling scores
6. Protection—keeping someone safe

**Secret**:
1. Hiding a criminal past
2. In debt to dangerous people
3. Knows something they shouldn't
4. Not who they claim to be
5. Betrayed someone they loved
6. Dying of illness/curse

### Physical Quirks (d8)
1. Missing fingers/ear
2. Distinctive scar
3. Nervous tic
4. Unusual eye color
5. Walks with a limp
6. Constantly chewing something
7. Speaks with hands
8. Never makes eye contact

### Voice/Speech Patterns
- Speaks slowly, choosing words carefully
- Fast talker, hard to interrupt
- Whispers everything
- Laughs at inappropriate times
- Uses elaborate vocabulary
- Speaks in third person
- Heavy accent
- Stutters when nervous

## Roleplay Guidelines

### Making NPCs Memorable

1. **Immediate Hook**: Something visible or audible that distinguishes them instantly
2. **Clear Want**: What do they want from this conversation/scene?
3. **Obstacle**: What's stopping them from getting it?
4. **Humanity**: One sympathetic or relatable detail

### Dialogue Principles

- **Don't monologue**: NPCs should respond, not lecture
- **React to players**: Adjust tone based on how PCs treat them
- **Information costs**: Important info requires persuasion, payment, or favors
- **Imperfect knowledge**: NPCs don't know everything; rumors can be wrong

### NPC Relationships

Track how NPCs feel about the party:
- **Hostile**: Will work against them, may attack
- **Unfriendly**: Won't help, might hinder
- **Indifferent**: No investment either way
- **Friendly**: Willing to help within reason
- **Allied**: Actively supports, takes risks for them

Actions shift relationships. Promises made should be tracked.

## Named NPC Template

For important recurring NPCs:

```markdown
## [Name]

**Role**: [Occupation/Position]
**Location**: [Where typically found]
**Appearance**: [Brief physical description]

**Personality**: [2-3 key traits]
**Motivation**: [What drives them]
**Secret**: [Hidden truth]
**Voice**: [How they speak]

**Relationship to Party**: [Current standing]
**Useful For**: [What they can offer PCs]

**Stats** (if needed):
- Use [X] stat block from SRD
- Or: AC [X], HP [X], Attack +[X] for [damage]
```

## Improvised NPCs

When players interact with an unexpected NPC:

1. **Assign immediate traits**: Pick one demeanor, one physical quirk
2. **Determine knowledge level**: What would they realistically know?
3. **Note them down**: If interaction is significant, record for consistency
4. **Give them a name**: Even minor NPCs become memorable with names

## Factions & Groups

When creating organizations:

1. **Goal**: What does the faction want?
2. **Methods**: How do they pursue it?
3. **Symbol**: Visual identifier
4. **Leader**: Named NPC at the top
5. **Agent types**: What members do PCs encounter?
6. **Resources**: What can they bring to bear?
7. **Enemies**: Who opposes them?
